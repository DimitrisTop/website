01 · Business Context

Timalfes is a premium brand operating in scientific cosmetology and pure apiary products. They had an established physical presence but no digital channel aligned with their brand quality. The project goal was not to build a website — it was to design and implement a customer-facing process that reflects how the business actually operates: from product selection and ordering through to fulfillment and post-sale communication.

02 · Process Mapping & Requirements

Before any implementation, I mapped the core business processes end-to-end:

  • Customer Journey: From first visit (product discovery) through selection, checkout, and order confirmation. Identified friction points in the default WooCommerce flow that needed to be resolved before launch.
  • Order Management Process: Mapped how orders enter the system, how inventory state changes, and what the operator needs to see to fulfill orders accurately — aligning the platform configuration with actual back-office workflow.
  • Catalog & Data Sync: Defined requirements for the product feed: which fields are mandatory, how pricing and availability propagate to advertising channels, and what the update cadence needs to be.

03 · Implementation & Integration

With the process requirements clear, implementation focused on aligning the platform with those needs:

  • Checkout Optimization: Refined the checkout flow to reduce steps and user confusion, directly reducing drop-off risk.
  • Full-Funnel Tracking: Configured GTM and Meta Pixel to track purchase events, cart additions, and checkout funnel stages — providing the data layer for future marketing decisions.
  • Catalog Automation: Set up an automated XML product feed to keep advertising channels synchronized with live inventory and pricing.

04 · Handover & Status

This was a fixed-scope digital transformation project. Following deployment, I structured the handover documentation to ensure the internal team could manage the platform independently — covering the admin workflows, tracking configuration, and catalog maintenance process.

Note: Active technical support concluded in early 2025. Current site performance reflects post-handover management.
